Winning Free software

Published: Sat 19 September 2015
By jaredj

In Commentary.

I've worried a lot lately rather than coding. I haven't got that much time to do either of them, so it's important to make sure that the coding I do helps people or has lasting merit. Or at least it seems like that.

This means I haven't got time to improve operating systems for mobile phones: by the time I did something good the phone would be obsolete. WebOS and Hellaphone, out the window. It means I haven't got time for Clojure, because to be effective all the software I wrote in that language would be GPL-incompatible. I can't use niche software, because nobody's watching for security bugs in it. And if I write some niche software, it's not helping the world nearly as much as I thought, because the world hasn't found it, and because it only helps people who already know how to use computers, i.e. white dudes my age. What I have released (CMITS) has lost to other things that were covered by press releases, like SIMP and SCAP. No mindshare, no community, no progress, lose.

Maybe if I find a LUG or some makers to hang out with, instead of just reading tech news all the time, things will get better.
