proquints vs. bubblebabble

Published: Fri 21 April 2017
By jaredj

In Commentary.

The Bubble Babble encoding, due to Antti Huima, was invented in 2000. It has six vowels and seventeen consonants, and checksums built in so you can tell whether a purported Bubble Babble string is valid or not. A Bubble Babble string always begins and ends with 'x'. This encoding is used in OpenSSH when you ssh-keygen -l -B.

The scheme of proquints ("PRO-nounceable QUINT-uplets") was introduced in 2009 by Daniel Shawcross Wilkerson. This scheme has four vowels, sixteen consonants, and no checksums. It takes more linguistics into account, avoiding homophones; it avoids the use of the character 'x', on account of its preexisting use to denote hexadecimal numbers. It is suggested that a proquint string start with '0q-'. Proquints are used in Upspin.

There's a pretty good Hacker News thread mentioning proquints and also Abbrase (from rmmh, a fork from bcaller).
