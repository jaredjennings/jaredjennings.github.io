Look on my works, ye mighty, and despair

Published: Sun 01 June 2014
By jaredj

In Commentary.

I've been learning about Linked Data lately, along with other related concepts like RDF, RDFS, OWL, and SPARQL, and having a lot of fun with it. I think it's quite promising, which is a silly statement to make because it's already doing a lot of work for many people: it's warning people about credit card fraud, cataloging sports stats, and composing DuckDuckGo zero-click results and Google Cards (kinda). It's been through three names (RDF, Semantic Web, Linked Data) already. But it seems like all the books written on it are ten years old, and some decent number of websites critical to its community are offline. After receiving a timeout while connecting to CKAN, I suddenly thought of Ozymandias. But perhaps the Kangbashi New Area is a better analogy: unlike Ozymandias' works, it still stands, but few inhabit it.
